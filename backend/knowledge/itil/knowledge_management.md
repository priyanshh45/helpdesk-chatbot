# Knowledge Management

## Purpose

Knowledge Management ensures that useful information is created, maintained, shared, and reused to improve IT support efficiency and service quality.

---

## Overview

Knowledge Management helps support teams solve issues faster by providing documented solutions, procedures, FAQs, troubleshooting guides, and best practices.

Instead of solving the same problem repeatedly, support engineers can use existing knowledge articles.

---

## Objectives

- Reduce resolution time.
- Improve first-contact resolution.
- Share knowledge across IT teams.
- Improve consistency in support.
- Reduce repeated work.

---

## Types of Knowledge

### Known Errors

Documented problems with temporary workarounds.

---

### SOPs

Standard Operating Procedures used to perform routine tasks.

---

### FAQs

Frequently asked questions from users.

---

### Troubleshooting Guides

Step-by-step procedures for resolving common issues.

---

### Technical Documentation

Configuration details, architecture diagrams, and installation procedures.

---

## Knowledge Article Structure

A good knowledge article should include:

- Title
- Purpose
- Symptoms
- Cause
- Resolution Steps
- Escalation
- Related Articles
- Keywords

---

## Benefits

- Faster ticket resolution
- Better user experience
- Reduced training time
- Consistent troubleshooting
- Improved collaboration

---

## Best Practices

- Keep articles updated.
- Use simple language.
- Include screenshots where applicable.
- Remove outdated information.
- Review articles regularly.
- Tag articles with keywords.

---

## Example

Problem:

VPN not connecting.

Knowledge Article:

1. Verify internet connection.
2. Restart VPN client.
3. Check credentials.
4. Verify VPN server status.
5. Contact Network Support if unresolved.
