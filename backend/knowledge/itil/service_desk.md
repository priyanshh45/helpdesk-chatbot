# Service Desk

## Purpose

The Service Desk serves as the single point of contact (SPOC) between users and the IT department. It is responsible for handling incidents, fulfilling service requests, providing technical assistance, and ensuring effective communication throughout the support process.

---

## Overview

The Service Desk is the first level of IT support. It receives requests from users through various communication channels such as phone calls, emails, chat, self-service portals, or ticketing systems.

The primary goal of the Service Desk is to restore normal service as quickly as possible while maintaining high customer satisfaction.

---

## Scope

The Service Desk handles:

- Incident logging
- Service request fulfillment
- Initial troubleshooting
- User communication
- Ticket prioritization
- Ticket escalation
- Ticket closure
- Knowledge sharing

---

## Responsibilities

### End User

- Report issues
- Provide required information
- Confirm issue resolution

### Service Desk Analyst

- Log tickets
- Categorize incidents
- Assign priorities
- Perform first-level troubleshooting
- Resolve common issues
- Escalate unresolved issues
- Update users on ticket progress

### Support Teams

- Resolve complex issues
- Provide technical expertise
- Update ticket status

---

## Service Desk Workflow

1. User reports an issue or request.
2. Service Desk logs the ticket.
3. Ticket is categorized.
4. Priority is assigned.
5. Initial troubleshooting is performed.
6. If resolved, ticket is closed.
7. If unresolved, ticket is escalated.
8. User confirms resolution.
9. Ticket is closed.

---

## Common Activities

- Reset passwords
- Unlock user accounts
- Install software
- Troubleshoot Outlook
- Resolve printer issues
- Configure VPN
- Assist with hardware problems
- Provide basic network support

---

## Best Practices

- Record every interaction.
- Use clear and professional communication.
- Follow SLA guidelines.
- Update ticket status regularly.
- Document resolutions.
- Use the knowledge base before escalating.
