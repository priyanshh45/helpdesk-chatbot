# Incident Management

## Purpose

The purpose of Incident Management is to restore normal IT services as quickly as possible while minimizing the impact on business operations.

---

## Overview

An incident is an unplanned interruption to an IT service or a reduction in the quality of an IT service.

Examples include:

- Printer offline
- Outlook not opening
- VPN not connecting
- Computer not starting
- Wi-Fi disconnected

Incident Management focuses on restoring service quickly rather than identifying the root cause.

---

## Incident Lifecycle

1. Incident Identification
2. Ticket Logging
3. Categorization
4. Prioritization
5. Initial Diagnosis
6. Investigation
7. Resolution
8. User Confirmation
9. Ticket Closure

---

## Priority Levels

### P1 – Critical

- Business-wide outage
- Server down
- Network outage

### P2 – High

- Department affected
- Multiple users impacted

### P3 – Medium

- Single user issue
- Limited business impact

### P4 – Low

- Minor inconvenience
- Cosmetic issue
- General information request

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### User

Reports the issue.

### Service Desk

Logs and investigates the incident.

### Support Team

Resolves technical issues.

### Incident Manager

Coordinates major incidents and communicates with stakeholders.

---

## Resolution Process

1. Verify the issue.
2. Collect relevant information.
3. Search the knowledge base.
4. Apply troubleshooting steps.
5. Confirm the issue is resolved.
6. Update the ticket.
7. Close the incident.

---

## Escalation

Escalate when:

- Resolution is beyond Service Desk capability.
- SLA is at risk.
- Major outage occurs.
- Vendor support is required.

---

## Best Practices

- Log every incident.
- Prioritize correctly.
- Keep users informed.
- Follow documented procedures.
- Meet SLA targets.
- Record the final resolution.
