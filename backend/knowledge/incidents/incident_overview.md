# Incident Overview

## Purpose

This document explains what an incident is, how incidents are handled, and the responsibilities of the IT Service Desk during the incident lifecycle.

---

## Definition

An incident is an unplanned interruption to an IT service or a reduction in the quality of an IT service.

The primary objective of Incident Management is to restore normal service as quickly as possible.

---

## Examples of Incidents

- Computer not starting
- Printer offline
- Outlook not opening
- VPN connection failure
- Network outage
- Blue Screen of Death (BSOD)
- Email not sending
- Shared drive inaccessible

## Incident Lifecycle

1. Incident Detection
2. Ticket Creation
3. Categorization
4. Priority Assignment
5. Investigation
6. Resolution
7. User Confirmation
8. Closure

---

## Information Required

When logging an incident, collect:

- User name
- Employee ID (if applicable)
- Device name
- Location
- Contact details
- Description of the issue
- Error message
- Time of occurrence
- Screenshots (if available)

---

## Responsibilities

### User

Reports the issue accurately.

### Service Desk

Logs, categorizes, prioritizes, and resolves incidents whenever possible.

### Technical Support Team

Handles complex technical issues.

---

## Ticket Closure

A ticket should only be closed after:

- The issue has been resolved.
- The user confirms the resolution.
- Resolution details are documented.

---

## Best Practices

- Log every incident.
- Gather complete information.
- Communicate clearly with users.
- Update tickets regularly.
- Follow SLA timelines.
