# Ticket Escalation SOP

## Purpose

This SOP explains when and how incidents should be escalated to ensure timely resolution and compliance with Service Level Agreements (SLAs).

---

## Overview

Escalation is the process of transferring an incident to a higher support level or another specialized team when it cannot be resolved by the current support engineer.

---

## Types of Escalation

### Functional Escalation

Transfer the ticket to a team with specialized technical knowledge.

Examples:

- Network Team
- Server Team
- Database Team
- Security Team

---

### Hierarchical Escalation

Notify management when:

- SLA is at risk.
- Business impact increases.
- Major incident occurs.

---

## Escalation Criteria

Escalate when:

- Issue cannot be resolved within the Service Desk.
- Administrator privileges are required.
- Hardware replacement is needed.
- Security incident is suspected.
- Multiple users are affected.
- SLA target is close to being breached.

---

## Escalation Workflow

1. Document troubleshooting already completed.
2. Update the incident ticket.
3. Assign the ticket to the appropriate support team.
4. Inform the user.
5. Monitor ticket progress.
6. Verify resolution before closure.

---

## Best Practices

- Never escalate without performing initial troubleshooting.
- Include screenshots and error messages.
- Record all actions taken.
- Keep the user informed throughout the process.
